Executive Summary

AtomsLearning faced a critical infrastructure challenge that many rapidly growing EdTech platforms encounter: balancing massive traffic spikes during exam periods while maintaining cost efficiency during quieter preparation cycles.

The existing AWS EC2 infrastructure couldn't adapt to these dramatic usage patterns, resulting in poor performance during peak times and unnecessary costs during low-traffic periods.

DeepTechLabs designed and implemented a dual-mode architecture that automatically transitions between high-performance EKS clusters during exams and cost-optimized K3s deployments during preparation periods.

The Challenge

Understanding the critical infrastructure pain points that were limiting platform growth

Peak Traffic Overwhelm

MockExam microservices experienced severe performance degradation during exam nights when student traffic surged unpredictably, creating critical service bottlenecks.

Infrastructure Rigidity

Static EC2 deployment architecture couldn't scale elastically, leading to over-provisioning during quiet periods and under-provisioning during traffic peaks.

Cost Inefficiency

High fixed infrastructure costs maintained year-round, despite traffic patterns showing clear seasonal variations in platform usage and demand cycles.

Original Architecture Limitations

Static EC2 Instances

Fixed capacity allocation regardless of actual demand patterns

Manual Scaling Operations

Reactive responses to traffic spikes with significant lead times

Monolithic Deployment

Limited ability to scale individual service components independently

Strategic Approach

Designing a dual-mode architecture optimized for distinct operational patterns

DeepTechLabs identified two critical operational modes requiring fundamentally different infrastructure approaches to optimize both performance and cost efficiency.

Rather than building one infrastructure to handle all scenarios, we designed two complementary systems that automatically transition based on demand patterns and scheduling, delivering optimal performance and cost efficiency for each operational mode.
